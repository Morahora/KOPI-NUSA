Kopi Aceh Gayo (Aceh Gayo Coffee)

In the westernmost area of ​​Indonesia, two types of coffee are produced, namely Arabica and Robusta coffee.

Well, what Aceh is most famous for is its Gayo Arabica coffee which is predicted to be one of the best coffees in the world. The strongest characteristic of Aceh Gayo coffee is its very sharp aroma. In addition, Gayo coffee does not leave a bitter aftertaste after drinking it, in contrast to most other types of coffee which leave a bitter aftertaste. This is the reason why many people really enjoy Aceh Gayo coffee.

Kopi Lampung (Lampung Coffee)

In contrast to Aceh Gayo, which is more famous for its Arabica coffee, Lampung coffee is actually very superior to Robusta coffee.

The most pronounced characteristic of Indonesian coffee from Lampung is its smooth texture, but quite strong taste. The dry processing method used in processing Lampung coffee beans is also believed to be the origin of the strong taste and characteristics in it.

Kopi Jawa (Java Coffee)

The production of Javanese coffee beans is generally carried out using the wet processing method

So that the taste may be slightly different and not as strong as the coffee beans produced in Sumatra or Sulawesi. Even so, this type of Arabica coffee is very much enjoyed because the taste is considered 'balanced'. The medium level of acidity and thickness that is not too thick and the resulting mild spice aroma makes it its own characteristic when you drink it.

Kopi Kintamani Bali

The characteristic of this Indonesian coffee is the fresh taste of sour (citrus) like oranges.

The aroma is considered exotic, complemented by a light texture, making this coffee not too bitter and does not leave a thick aftertaste afterwards. So, because of that, this type of coffee might be enjoyed more by people who don't really like to drink coffee with a 'heavy' body.

Kopi Flores Bajawa

This Arabica coffee from Flores Bajawa produces medium acidity and a light taste texture.

Apart from its tantalizing aroma, the characteristics of this coffee are also known for its sweet sensation as well as the taste of nuts and herbs in it. This uniqueness that you may not be able to enjoy in other coffees. It's no wonder that this type of Flores Bajawa can penetrate the international market because of these advantages, right?

Kopi Papua Wamena

The sharpness of the aroma with a light taste is the hallmark of this archipelago coffee from the eastern part of Indonesia.

Similar to Balinese coffee which has a floral taste, Papua Wamena coffee is also complemented by fragrant nuances of chocolate and herbs. The 'smokey' aftertaste after drinking it is also its own characteristic and uniqueness. Its soft, non-greasy texture is also very friendly in the mouth.

Kopi Toraja

Having another name Celebes Kalossi, this coffee from the Sulawesi region has a very distinctive and fragrant aroma.

What makes it quite likable is the low acidity level. The uniqueness of the characteristics of Toraja coffee lies in the tendency of the resulting floral and fruity flavors. In addition, the strong and slightly sour coffee taste leaves a unique aftertaste on the tongue.
